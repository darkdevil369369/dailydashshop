Prioritize Clinical Skills & Hard Metrics

Don't just list duties; quantify your impact. Instead of 'Provided patient care,' write 'Managed caseload of 6-8 critical care patients, maintaining a 98% medication administration accuracy rate.' Include specific EMR systems (Epic, Cerner, Meditech) and equipment proficiencies (IV pumps, ventilators, defibrillators).

Showcase Certifications & Licenses Prominently

List all relevant licenses (RN, BSN) and certifications (BLS, ACLS, PALS, NIHSS, CCRN, CEN, OCN) in a dedicated, easy-to-find section. For ATS, spell out full certification names initially, followed by the acronym in parentheses, e.g., 'Advanced Cardiovascular Life Support (ACLS).'

Tailor Your Resume to Each Job Description

Review the job posting for keywords related to patient populations (e.g., 'pediatric oncology,' 'geriatric behavioral health'), specific units (e.g., 'Emergency Department,' 'Surgical ICU'), and required skills. Mirror their language in your experience and skills sections to pass ATS filters and show direct relevance.

Highlight Soft Skills with Clinical Context

While 'communication' is vital, demonstrate it through action: 'Facilitated interdisciplinary rounds to improve patient discharge planning by 15%.' Emphasize critical thinking: 'Utilized clinical judgment to rapidly assess deteriorating patient status and initiate appropriate interventions per protocol, leading to improved outcomes.'

Proofread Meticulously for ATS & Recruiters

A single typo can disqualify an otherwise strong candidate, especially in a field where precision is paramount. Beyond spell-check, ensure consistent formatting, proper capitalization for medical terms, and that all dates and contact information are accurate. Use a clean, professional font like Calibri or Arial.

FAQ

Should I include my GPA on my nursing resume?

Only include your GPA if you are a recent graduate (within 1-2 years) and your GPA is 3.5 or higher. Otherwise, focus on clinical rotations, preceptorships, and relevant work experience.

How long should a nurse's resume be?

For nurses with less than 10 years of experience, one page is ideal. If you have extensive experience, certifications, and leadership roles, a two-page resume is acceptable, but ensure every bullet point adds significant value.

What's the best way to list my clinical rotations?

For new grads, dedicate a 'Clinical Experience' section. List the facility, unit (e.g., Med-Surg, ICU), hours, and 2-3 bullet points detailing specific skills or patient care provided. For experienced nurses, this can be condensed or omitted in favor of paid work experience.
